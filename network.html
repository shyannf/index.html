Term Definition
Network Classification - A computer network is often classified as being a LAN, MAN, or a WAN.
  • LAN- (Local Area Network) that normally consists of a collection of computers in a single building or complex.
  • MAN- (Metropolitan Area Network) of intermediate size, such as one spanning a local community.
  • WAN- (Wide Area Network) that links a machine over a greater distance- perhaps in neighboring cities or on opposite sides of the world.
    • Network Models - A network model is a database model that is designed as a flexible approach to representing objects and their relationships.
  • Bus Network- A group of machines that are all connected to a common communication line called a bus.
  • Star Network- The star is one machine that serves as a central focal point to which others are connected too.
    • Protocols A protocol is established rules that help the netork to function properly, these rules are established for the activities to conduct.
  • CSMA/CD Ethernet- Carrier Sense, Multiple Access with Collision Detection dictates that each messsage be broadcast to all machines on the bus.
  • CSMA/CA Wifi- Carrier Sense, Multiple Access with Collision Avoidance are designed to avoid collision and may not eliminate them completely.
    • Combining Networks - Sometimes it is necessary to connect existing networks to form an extended communication system.
  • Repeater- This is a little more than a device that passes signzls back and forth between the two original buses without considering the meaning of the signals.
  • Bridge- Like a repeater it connects two buses and then looks at the destinationaddress that accompanies each message and fowards a message across the connection only when that message is destined for a computer on the other side.
  • Switch- A bridge with multiple connections, allowing it to connect several buses rather than two.
  • Router- Special purpose computers used for fowarding messages.
  • Gateway- The "point" at which one network is linked to an internet, it serves as a passageway between the network and the outside world.

    • Internet Four-layer - A collection of software routines.
  • Application Layer- Consist of those software units such as clients and servers that use Internet communication to carry out their tasks, uses the transport layer to send and recieve messages over the Internet.
  • Transport Layer- Adds sequence numbers to the small segments it produces so that the segments can be reassembled at the messages destination, accepts the messages from the application layer.
  • Network Layer- Its job is to decide in which direction a packet should be sent at each step along the packets path through the Internet, recognizes that the packet is complete.
  • Link Layer- Has the responsibility of transferring the packet to the last network layer.
    • Internet is: - A vast network that connects computers all over the world.
  • Packet-Switching Network- A type of computer communications network that groups and sends data in the form of small packets.
  • Fault Tolerant- The property that enables a system to continue operating properly in the event of the failure of one or more faults within some of its components.
  • Redunant- Something that is repeated unnecessarily or something that is not useful because there is already another or more advanced version.
    • Cybersecurity - The study of protecting computers, networks, and data from such attacks.
  • Malware- A malicious software attack that could be transferred to, and executed on, the computer itself, or it might attack the computer from a distance.
  • Virus- A software that infects a computer by inserting itself into programs that already reside in the machine.
  • Worm- A autonomous program that transfers itself through a network, taking up residence in computers and fowarding copies itself to other computers.
  • Trojan Horse- A progran that enters a computer system disguised as a desirable program, such as a game or useful utility package, that is willingly imported by a victim.
  • Spyware- A malicious software that collects information about activities at the computer on which it resides and reports that information back to the instigator of the attack.
  • Phishing- A technique of obtaining information explicitly by simply asking for it.
  • DDoS Attack- Generates the attacks is distributed over many hosts.

  • Antivirus Software- Used to detect and remove the presence of known viruses of known viruses and other infections.
  • Cryptography- The study of sending and recieving messages securely in the presences of adversaries.
